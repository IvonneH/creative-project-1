Snow on the Window Pane

Snow on the window pane is dark, menacing
A picture of the impact frozen in time.
And on the window pane.
A great gray mess, it taunts you with imagined faces.
Uncaring that it distracts from your view.

Snow on the window pane is fun, magical.
A picture of the impact frozen in time.
And in the child’s memory.
A shining array, it’s looked upon by smiling faces.
Playing outside, they enjoy the view.

Dear the Color Blue,

You are my favorite.
My Dad told me
You are a gift of love
From God.
You greet the sun and encircle the earth each new day,
Shining on my face, into my eyes,
Penetrating my soul as I look up.
I carry you with me always.
I study the image in the mirror critically, noting imperfections,
Then I see in my eyes,
That love.
The sky,
Ocean,
My backpack,
That pencil,
That paint,
Those flowers,
My favorite T-shirts I wear
And people notice my eyes
Speaking kind words
Spreading God’s love.
Thank you, Blue,
For being a feeling far reaching
As the sky,
And overwhelming
As the ocean.

Signed,
Your Dear Friend
Vonnie
